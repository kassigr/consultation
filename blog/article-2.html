Creating Boundaries in Remote Work

November 5, 2025

Remote work offers flexibility, but it can also blur the lines between professional and personal life. Here are some tips for creating healthy boundaries:

1. Define Your Workspace

Designate a specific area in your home for work. This helps create a psychological boundary between your work and personal life. When you leave your workspace, you're "leaving work."

2. Stick to a Routine

Start and end your workday at a consistent time. A morning routine can help you transition into work mode, and an evening routine can help you switch off.

3. Communicate Your Availability

Let your colleagues know your working hours and when you'll be unavailable. This manages expectations and prevents you from feeling like you need to be "on" all the time.

4. Take Real Breaks

Step away from your computer for lunch and short breaks. Don't eat at your desk. Use this time to recharge and disconnect from work-related tasks.

5. Turn Off Notifications

After your workday is over, turn off notifications for work-related apps on your phone and computer. This is crucial for protecting your personal time and reducing the temptation to check in.
